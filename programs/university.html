Five Executive Competencies

Skills that define executive-level communicators

1. The Elevator Pitch

Distill complex ideas into compelling 30-second, 60-second, and 3-minute narratives. Hook your audience from the first word.

  • • Crafting the hook
  • • Clear value proposition
  • • Call to action

2. Crisis Communication

Stay calm under pressure. Communicate clearly in high-stakes situations. Manage reputation and deliver difficult messages with tact.

  • • Managing emotional responses
  • • Delivering bad news
  • • Reputation management

3. Negotiation & Persuasion

Win-win negotiations, persuasion psychology, and relationship-building. Get to yes without compromising your values.

  • • Principled negotiation
  • • Persuasion techniques
  • • Building rapport

4. Diplomatic Discourse

Navigate sensitive conversations, cultural nuances, and cross-border communication with grace and effectiveness.

  • • Cross-cultural communication
  • • Tactful disagreement
  • • Protocol & etiquette

5. Executive Presence

Command respect when you enter a room. Project confidence, gravitas, and authority without arrogance.

  • • Body language & posture
  • • Vocal authority
  • • Personal branding

8-Week Intensive Curriculum

Fast-track your executive communication skills

Week 1: Foundation & Presence

The Power of First Impressions: Executive presence fundamentals, body language mastery, vocal authority, commanding the room, introduction round-robin with feedback

Week 2: The Elevator Pitch

Hook, Value, Action: Crafting 30-sec, 60-sec, 3-min pitches, storytelling structures, compelling openings, clear value propositions, practice pitches with peer feedback

Week 3: Negotiation Skills

Getting to Yes: Principled negotiation framework, BATNA (Best Alternative), win-win mindset, persuasion psychology, role-play negotiations

Week 4: Crisis Communication

Grace Under Pressure: Managing high-stakes conversations, delivering bad news, crisis response frameworks, emotional regulation, simulated crisis scenarios

Week 5: Leading Meetings

Facilitation Mastery: Running effective meetings, managing difficult participants, time management, conflict resolution, decision-making frameworks

Week 6: Diplomatic Discourse

Cultural Intelligence: Cross-cultural communication, international etiquette, navigating sensitive topics, tactful disagreement, diplomatic language

Week 7: Capstone Preparation

Showcase Readiness: Developing capstone presentation, integrating all skills, rehearsal sessions, peer consultation, refinement based on feedback

Week 8: Capstone Presentations & Certification

Grand Finale: Final capstone presentations to panel of professionals, peer evaluations, professional feedback, certificate ceremony, networking reception

Final Project

The Capstone Presentation

Your capstone is a 10-minute professional presentation on a topic of your choice—whether it's a business pitch, policy proposal, or thought leadership talk.

You'll present to a panel of business leaders, receive professional feedback, and have your presentation recorded for your portfolio.

Capstone Options:
  • • Investor pitch for your startup idea
  • • Policy proposal presentation
  • • Thought leadership TEDx-style talk
  • • Product launch presentation
  • • Conference keynote simulation
